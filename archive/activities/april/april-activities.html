Lynn's Roasted Red Potatoes
Ingredients
4 cups chopped/halved red potatoes
1 1/2 pounds
1/4 cup olive oil
3 tablespoons parmesan cheese
1 teaspoon garlic salt
1 teaspoon dried basil
1 teaspoon dried parsley
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
Instructions
Heat oven to 400°. Spray a cooking sheet with cooking spray. Set aside.
Add the chopped/halved red potatoes, olive oil, parmesan cheese, garlic salt, dried basil, dried parsley, salt, and pepper into a mixing bowl. Stir together until all the potatoes are coated.
Spread the potatoes evenly onto the cookie sheet making sure that there are no potatoes that are overlapping. For the best roasted potatoes you want them spread out so all sides can crisp up.
Cook for 30-35 minutes, stirring halfway through the cook time.
The cook time may vary depending on the size of your potato chunks. Mine cook for 30 minutes and they are roughly 1-inch sized pieces. If you like the potatoes really crispy then cook for even longer depending on preference.
When you stir the potatoes halfway through the cook time, use a spatula, and know that some potatoes may be stuck (slightly) to the cookie sheet, this is totally fine and they will come off with a spatula. It's just the parmesan cheese sticking. This is also why you need to stir them halfway through, so that all sides can crisp up, and so none are sticking to the pan for the whole cook time.
Notes
I like to use the small petite baby red potatoes. They are small in size so you really only have to cut each one in half. For larger ones maybe cut into fourths. You can use regular red potatoes but just know that not all the pieces will have skin (which the skin gets roasted and crispy so it's really the best part.

Kenzie's Instant Pot Garlic Roasted Potatoes
 ¼ cup olive oil
1 ½ pounds russet potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
1 teaspoon garlic powder
1 teaspoon fine sea salt
½ teaspoon thyme
½ teaspoon dried rosemary
¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
1 cup vegetable broth
Step 1
Turn on a multi-functional pressure cooker (such as Instant Pot®) and select Saute function. Heat olive oil and add potatoes; cook until lightly golden, 5 to 8 minutes.
Step 2
Sprinkle garlic powder, salt, thyme, rosemary, and pepper over potatoes. Pour in vegetable broth. Close and lock the lid. Select high pressure according to manufacturer's instructions; set timer for 7 minutes. Allow 10 to 15 minutes for pressure to build.
Step 3
Release pressure carefully using the quick-release method according to manufacturer's instructions, about 5 minutes.
Unlock and remove lid

1 large onion_
4 cloves garlic

1 poblano chili

Whitney's Yum Yum Potatoes Recipe
2 pounds russet potatoes (peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes)
2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
1 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
3 large eggs (hard-boiled and finely chopped)
1 small red onion finely diced)
1/2 large dill pickle (finely diced)
1 cup celery (finely diced)
2 tablespoons fresh dill (finely chopped)
1/4 cup green onions finely chopped)

Dressing:
2 cloves garlic (minced)
1/2 cup mayonnaise
1/2 cup sour cream
3 tablespoons pickle juice
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:
Wash, peel, and cube the potatoes. Add them to a large pot over high heat and add enough water that would cover the potatoes about 1 1/2 inches.

Once the water starts boiling, add 1 teaspoon of salt, and cook for 12-15 minutes, just until fork tender. Be careful not to over-cook the potatoes.

Add the cooked potatoes to a colander and drain. Transfer to a large bowl and drizzle with 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar and let them cool to room temperature.

Finely dice the cooled eggs and add to the cooled potatoes. Add celery, red onion green onion pickle, and fresh dill. In a medium bowl mix all the Salad Dressing ingredients until fully combined. Pour over the potatoes and gently mix, careful to not entirely crush the potatoes.

Cover and refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours before serving. The potatoes will harden a little once they are cooled. Garnish with dill, green onions and pickles before serving.

Lau's Fried Mashed Potato Puffs

INGREDIENTS
650 g of potatoes (around 7 mid-sized yellow potatoes or 10 smaller red ones)
2 egg yolks
Flour
Cooking oil
Salt
Pepper

PROCEDURE
1. Peel the potatoes and cut into slices. Put into a cooking pot with cold water and lots of salt. The water should be completely covering the potatoes. Cook over mid-high heat. When the water starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and leave cooking for 15 minutes or until the potatoes are cooked.
2. Take out the potatoes and drain them, leave them out a few minutes until they dry a bit.
3. Put cooking oil into the pan (about two fingers tall) and heat up until it's reached 180 ºC. Do not put the potatoes in yet.
4. Take the potatoes out and MASH THEM into purée, you can use your hands or a potato masher. Put the mashed potatoes in a bowl and add some salt and pepper. Add the two egg yolks into the bowl and knead until it's homogeneous. Use a spoon to take a portion and make this portion into a cute potato sphere (should be the size of a golf sphere). Cover with flour. Then repeat several times until you run out and are only left with potato spheres.
5. Pre-heat your oven to 100 ºC (or 200 ºF) only if you need to keep the potatoes warm until they're served.
6. Use the pan to fry about 8 potatoes at a time (depending on your pan size). The potatoes should be in for about 3 minutes until they're golden and pretty. If a potato sphere starts opening up, remove from oil immediately. Put the fried puffs on absorbent paper to remove the extra oil.
7. Serve them hot!

NOTES
You can't make them in the oven, it doesn't work.
You can't replace the flour with panko or such.
Some people like adding sugar to the puffs, but I personally do not like them sweet.
I had to use 'spheres' because of the Neopets filters.


Vanessa's Japanese Potato Salad

Ingredients
300g russet potatoes
50g carrot
80g canned corn
50g onion_
50g cucu.mber
2 slices ham
4 tbsp Japanese mayonnaise
1/2 tbsp rice vinegar
salt and pepper

Instructions
Thinly slice the cucu.mber. Sprinkle the cucu.mber with salt and squeeze out the excess water with a paper towel.
Slice ham into 1cm square pieces. Dice the carrot and onion_
Cut the potato into small pieces, wash, add water until covered and add 1/2 tsp of salt. Boil potatoes until cooked.
Drain and mash the potatoes with a fork.
Season the potatoes with the rice wine vinegar, and salt and pepper. Leave to cool in the fridge.
When cool, combine potatoes with all the other ingredients. Serve chilled and enjoy
