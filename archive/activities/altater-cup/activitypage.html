Altater Cup Crossword ◈



It's time for an Altater Cup Crossword! It's rather difficult, and you will need to look far and wide for the answers. But we guarantee that they are all discoverable with a bit of digging!

If you get all 15 answers correct, you will receive 750 points for your Altater team.
If you get at least 10 answers correct, you will receive 500 points for your Altater team.
If you get at least 5 answers correct, you will receive 250 points for your Altater team.

To submit your answers, send a neomail to Kat with your completed crossword hosted on a petpage (or send the crossword image in another way that is easy for you). You are only allowed one (1) submission. Please refrain from sharing answers with your Altater teammates and rivals.

Marathons ◈



This activity is over! Thanks for playing!



Throughout the Altater Cup, we will have members hosting marathons where everyone plays one game at the same time for 30 mins with the hopes of reaching a group goal. These marathons will be hosted on the guild board!

For participating in a marathon, you will receive 100 Altater Cup points, and if the group hits the goal in the 30 min, everyone gets an extra 100 points each! (Hosts can award 50 bonus points for reaching 150% of the goal, and 100 bonus points for reaching 200% of the goal. But please don't set low goals on purpose just to get to these bonus points—goals should be realistic.)

Some marathons will be scheduled in advance, while others can be a little more spontaneous. The only rule is that you need at least 3 people in attendance to have a spontaneous marathon!

Want to host a marathon? Awesome! Send Kat a neomail with a date, time, and game you want to play. It will then be added to the list below. Make sure it’s a good time you’re available to be on the guild boards for the whole 30 mins and a little time after to add up the points. You don't need to decide on a goal in advance - once the marathon is starting, you can assess how many people will be participating, and set a goal as a group.

At the end of a marathon, whether you hosted one that was scheduled or spontaneous, mail Kat again to let her know who participated, what the goal was, and the end total!

Altater Cup History ◈



For 21 years, Neopia was peaceful. Kacheeks could count potatoes, Acaras could watch birds peacefully, Blumaroos could have picnics in the park with their best Draik friends, daydreaming together, lost in reverie. But in Y22, there was a FLASH and Neopia's lands shifted violently. The world shook, borders were ripped apart, nearby planets and spacecrafts came crashing down, and strangely potatoes rained from the sky and flew everywhere.

When the dust settled, nothing looked as it did before. The survivors gathered and decided to recreate the world, to make it better than ever before. They named the new world Exodus, in honour of their departure from the old society. With everything in disarray, they split apart to create four new lands and bring order to the world once more. And so Kreluquan Mountain, Mystidel Desert, Meroonia and Krawken Woods were born.

There was peace amongst the lands as they all learned how to live in their new environments together, and how to eat potatoes about 65 different ways. But to maintain a slice of their Neopian heritage, the four lands would still meet every summer for the time-honored tradition of competing in yooyuball, slinging slushies, and screaming at the top of their lungs. They called it The Altater Cup.



Kreluquan Mountain


At the very top of the new world is Kreluquan Mountain. Here the citizens of Kreludor, Maraqua and Terror Mountain created a haven they could call home. Their land reaches from space itself all the way to the depths of the sea floor. With the drastic topography it is no surprise that this land is considered to have the most diverse set of inhabitants. Thanks to the tonnes of snow and ice from the mountain not only do they specialize in Potato Slushies but during the Spring Melt they play host to the longest water slide in Exodus!

Mystidel Desert


To the East lays the Mystidel Desert. This land was claimed by Mystery Island, Darigan Citadel, and the Lost Desert. Mystidel Desert is a vast desert above which floats an island oasis. While the weather on the ground is typical for a desert, the island is quite unusual and, quite frankly, mysterious. From the time the sun rises until it sets, the sun is out, and it's warm with a nice breeze. Once night falls, however, a gloomy rain sets in that carries the island oasis through the night. Every night. But the citizens don't mind the rain because not only does it keep the raining potatoes at bay, but it also replenishes their water sources—the cleanest water on Exodus.



font requires NB Pen




Meroonia


Down South the land became known as Meroonia. Created by the former inhabitants of Meridell, Roo Island and Tyrannia, Meroonia is the only island within Exodus' ocean. Half of the land is dedicated farmland, where potatoes are grown along with other crops salvaged from the aftermath of The Flash. As the only farmland on Exodus, this food is exported throughout the world. The rest of the island is a large rock quarry where citizens live in brightly coloured stone houses, and meet up at the community concert hall to tell jokes and share puns.



font requires NB Pen




Krawken Woods


And finally, in the West Krawken Woods was born. This is where those of Krawk Island and the Haunted Woods settled down. Krawken Woods is a large, dense forest with countless deep rivers flowing throughout. The inhabitants live and navigate the land through the interlocking treetops. The rivers are filled with their expertly crafted potato-shaped pirate ships that are used to transport large groups or heavy items (and occasionally cause a bit of ruckus just to liven things up).
