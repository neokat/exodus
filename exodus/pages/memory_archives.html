Once upon a time, there was a sad, lonely purple kacheek. Her sadness stemmed from the fact that she was never given a name from her Neopian master before she was abandoned in the Lost Desert. Without a name, nobody wanted to adopt her or care for her. So, one day, she decided to go on a journey to find the perfect name.

She first went to the Shrine of Coltzan, to recieve wisdom from the great king; but all he gave her was a Sand Pita, so she went on her way. What does he know anyway, he was poisoned at his own dinner table, probably by a Sand Pita, no less. -Marie, blestdeath

Disappointed, she left Coltzan's Shrine and headed to Faerieland to seee someone else that she thought would be able to empathize with her: The Discarded Magical Blue Grundo Plushie of Prosperity. She approached the plushie, but it was too sad to talk, and left her a Pile of Bones instead... ominous. - Kat, superkathiee

With the pile of bones in mind, she decided it was time to visit the Deserted Fairground in the Haunted Woods. Perhaps, she can find nameless creatures like herself there? She then came across Castle Nox. -Ariana, lockheearts

Hubrid Nox wasn't of any help either. He just threw our sad, lonely purple kacheek back into the streets. It was, in the middle of the night, when she realized, she hasn't eaten in days. - Misa, d0odleb3rry

Our lonely purple kacheek walked around in the dark for a bit until she noticed a very delicious smell lingering in the air. Being as hungry as she was, she set out to track down where this intoxicating smell was coming from. It was then she saw the big cauldron sitting outside; being kept warm by an inviting fire. - Kelly, hilary_duff_fan_16

The lonely purple kacheek then looked up to see a faerie smiling at her. The faerie asked the purple kacheek if she would like a bowl of soup. The lonely purple kacheek nodded shyly as the faerie handed her a warm bowl of Potato and Sweetcorn Soup. - Melly, xomeepit

The timid kacheek hurriedly took to the Potato and Sweetcorn Soup with eyes that sparkled like the cosmos. As she ravenously slurped and chewed each bite, an idea began to formulate in her head. - Cory, xxsitrus

With a full belly, the purple Kacheek ventured to Faerieland, in hopes of meeting the grey faerie. Surely, an unnamed faerie with no powers can empathize and give me my name! - Christy, merpiie

Upon finding the grey faerie, the sad, lonely purple kacheek felt some how sadder. She nervously approached, and whispered, "hello, I am... well you see that is the problem. I am no one, and no one is me" The grey faerie looked deep into her eyes and said... -Vee, xeyota__fh

Oh, hullo." The grey faerie almost manages a smile for you. "Don't suppose you can find me a Mallard?"

Hello, Ms. Faerie.. perhaps you can help me. I've been wandering for days and I'm in search of a name." The Grey Faerie took this as an opportunity. "If you can find me a Mallard, I'll give you a clue..." so off the little kacheek went, to ask, I don't know who. - Misa, d0odleb3rry

Our sad kacheek heroine walked and walked, until she found herself somewhere in Meridell, where she happened upon a field of potatoes and in the distance she spotted... another kacheek, someone just like her! Maybe this bright, yellow kacheek didn't know his name either! She walked over to the farmer, who spots her and says "hi, friend! I'm Alton, a simple farmer and potato counter. What's your name?" Our heroine sighed. - Kat, superkathiee

A... name? I don't have such a thing," explained the purple kacheek as she fidgeted with her tail. The farmer suddenly shrieked at the top of his lungs saying something about how he names all his potatoes before pelting various sized spuds in all directions. - Cory, xxsitrus

With potatoes, carrots, and turnips flying in all directions, he shouted quite manically, "See here, this is Ole Sweetie and this is McSpuddington. Tell you what, I can tell you are down on your luck, so if you can beat my simple little game, I'll help you little whipper-snapper out." - Ice, honorrolle

And just like that, the lonely kacheek began playing Extreme Potato Counter. She then gets whacked in the head by a flying carrot and potato. She can't remember how she got here. Could she have gotten some amnesia? - Ariana, lockheearts

Alton came running over to her as our lonely kacheek came to. "Tatum!" He shouted, "are you okay, darlin?"

"I—What did you call me?!" she asked, staring from Alton to the potato that appeared in her hand.

"Tatum! Why, that's your name of course. Why are you looking at me like I have three heads? Don't you remember me?"

Tatum. Suddenly, all of her memories came flooding back. She used to spend afternoons on a farm just like this one, counting potatoes and dodging flying carrots. Alton himself looked oddly..... familiar. "Say, Alton, have we met before?"

"Have we met?" Alton let out the biggest belly laugh. "Have we met! Ha! Tatum, you're my best potato counter on this farm! You're the only reason I stay in business!"

"Then why did you... introduce yourself to me like we didn't know each other????" Tatum looked at him quizzically.

"Oh, you know, as a fellow kacheek, I just like to be a little.... cheeky." Alton grinned. "I last saw you on a truck headed to the Lost Desert with a shipment of my potatoes. You must have gotten hit in the head with one, that was the source of your amnesia!"

Tatum remembered that truck ride now, and waking up next to Coltzan's Shrine. Everything made sense now, and she felt content, remembering her place in Neopia. It was on this farm, surrounded by potatoes. Tatum was happy.
